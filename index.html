Euler's Formula

One of the most beautiful and profound equations in mathematics

eiπ + 1 = 0

What is Euler's Formula?

Euler's Formula establishes a deep relationship between fundamental constants of mathematics:

  • e: The base of natural logarithms (~2.718).
  • i: The imaginary unit, satisfying i² = -1.
  • π: The mathematical constant Pi (~3.14159).
  • 1 and 0: The multiplicative and additive identities, respectively.

Why is it Beautiful?

Euler's Formula elegantly combines five seemingly unrelated constants into a single, simple equation.

Applications

The formula is widely used in engineering, physics, and complex number theory to describe oscillations, waves, and exponential growth.
